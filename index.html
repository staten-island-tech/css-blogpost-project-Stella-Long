Iconic Asian Desserts

Mango Sago

Mango sago is a creamy, tropical Asian dessert that combines sweet mangoes with chewy tapioca (sago) pearls and coconut milk. This refreshing, chilled dish is widely popular in Hong Kong, and variations are found throughout Southeast Asia, including in Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines.

Pineapple Bun

A pineapple bun, or bo lo bao in Cantonese, is a sweet Hong Kong pastry with a soft, fluffy bread and a signature crunchy, golden-brown crust. Despite its name, the traditional version does not contain pineapple; it's named for the decorative, cracked pattern of the topping, which resembles the skin of a pineapple.

Egg Tart

An egg tart is a small, sweet dessert consisting of a pastry crust filled with a delicate egg custard. While there are regional variations, the most widely known versions originate from Portuguese and Cantonese culinary traditions. These desserts are typically served at Chinese bakeries and dim sum restaurants.
